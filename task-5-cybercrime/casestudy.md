# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. This usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or message that looks legitimate, such as a bank notification or job offer. The message contains a link that directs the user to a fake website. When the user enters their login details, the attacker captures this information and uses it for unauthorized access.

Students and young internet users are often targeted because they may not be aware of such scams. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking on links and sharing information online. Awareness and preventive measures can help reduce the risk of falling victim to phishing attacks.
